A Brief History About Ghana

Ghana became the first sub-Saharan African nation to gain independence from colonial rule on March 6, 1957,punder the leadership of Kwame Nkrumah, a key figure in the Pan-African movement. Nkrumah became Ghana's first president and sought to modernize the country through ambitious development projects,though his government was overthrown in 1966. Ghana experienced a series of military coups over the following decades. Stability was restored in the 1990s, and Ghana has since developed a reputation for its democratic governance and economic progress. Today, Ghana is known for its rich cultural heritage, vibrant democracy, and as a major producer of cocoa and gold.

A Brief History About Education in Ghana

Post-independence reforms sought to improve educational quality and access, though challenges such as inadequate infrastructure, teacher shortages, and regional disparities persisted.
